Back Pain Guide

Research-backed recovery · Sources: PureGym, Ironman Mag, ANSSI Wellness, r/fitness
⚡ Quick Rules
Don't sit OR stand for more than 30-60 min — alternate
Short 10-15 min walks between position changes
DOMS from squats/deadlifts resolves in 48-72 hours
Movement is the fix, not rest — don't stay in bed
If pain persists beyond 72 hours or is sharp/shooting → see a doctor
✅ Safe Exercises (No Lower Back Stress)
Lat PulldownSeated, thighs locked under pad, zero lower back load
Preacher CurlSeated, chest supported against pad
Skull CrushersLying flat on bench — back fully supported
Pec DeckSeated, back against pad
Rear Delt Fly (machine)Seated, chest against pad
⚠️ Safe BUT Watch Form
Cable RowKeep chest up, don't round back, lighter weight. Use chest-supported row machine if available
Shoulder PressDo it SEATED with back support (incline bench), NOT standing
🚫 Avoid With Back Pain
Squats / DeadliftsHeavy spinal compression — wait 48-72 hours
Bent Over Barbell RowHeavy lower back load in bent position
Standing OHPCompresses spine under load
Any heavy standing braceStresses already sore erector spinae muscles
🪑 Sitting vs Standing
Sitting
Compresses spinal discs
Tightens hip flexors
Less leg fatigue
Standing
Decompresses spine
Engages core gently
Causes fatigue if too long
Alternating
Best of both
50% less back pain (UT Southwestern study)
Prevents stiffness
Sources: UT Southwestern, PureGym, Ironman Magazine, ANSSI Wellness, Fitbod, r/fitness
